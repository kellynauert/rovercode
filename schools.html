Rovercode for Schools

The Rovercode Experience

For after-school and out-of-school Indiana academic standards-aligned programming. You can choose any or all of the following options to build an instant Rovercode experience for your students:

The Cutebot

Cutebot is a rear-drive robot that makes learning about hardware, software, and robotics fun when powered by the BBC micro:bit. This part of the Rovercode Experience is a robot that bluetooth connects to students’ laptops so they can interact with it through graphical programming blocks. This supports students to investigate programming from simple tasks to more complex problem solving. They can achieve distance tracking, obstacle avoidance, line following, wireless control, create light shows, and play music all through the Rovercode Experience modules.

The Nibble

Nibble is an educational tool that makes learning about electronics and programming fun! This part of the Rovercode Experience is a gaming console that students build using simple tools. After building, the Nibble comes preloaded with 4 retro games inspired by cult classics — Bonk, Invaderz, Snake, and SpaceRocks. Students, after successfully finishing the modules of the Nibbler, will have the opportunity to design and code their own interactive games.

HiWonder Qdee

Hiwonder Qdee is a programmable STEAM educational robotic kit powered by the micro:bit. This part of the Rovercode Experience is about customization and building. The Qdee can be equipped with a variety of electronic modules and metal brackets, and can be built into many form factors and programmable actions. The Rovercode Experience with Qdee teaches and inspires logical analysis, programming capability, multi-disciplinary thinking and scientific innovation.
